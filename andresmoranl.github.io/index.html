    # Mathematics

    Currently, I am looking for further improvements at the software-level of my program and the implementation of resolutions over the secondary Steenrod algebra, together with their synthetic interpretation.

    Some assets resulting from my Master's thesis include [\(\mathrm{mod} \; 2\)] (demo for mobile users) an extension of the minimal free \(\mathcal{A}_2-\)resolution of \(\mathbb{F}_2\) given by Hatcher's spectral sequences book, computed with a Python script made by me, except for some of SageMath's \(\mathrm{mod} \; 2\) linear algebra routines which are a significant bottleneck and will be replaced in further implementations, aiming to include GPU acceleration (CUDAMatrix). The script is a naive implementation of the process described in Hatcher's book, so it is far from being optimal because I am not considering more subtle aspects of the \(\mathrm{mod} \; 2\) Steenrod Algebra \( \mathcal{A}_2 \), such as its combinatorics as well as the vanishing lines of its subalgebras. Here, you can find a plot that resembles the \( E_2 \) page of the Adams spectral sequence for the sphere (for \(p = 2, 3, 5, 7\)), computed using my program (minr.py).

    The analogous computations can be found in [\(\mathrm{mod} \; 3\)], [\(\mathrm{mod} \; 5\)] and [\(\mathrm{mod} \; 7\)]. Note the sparseness effect induced on the \(E_2-\)page of the Adams Spectral Sequence by taking increasing prime numbers. That suggests (correctly) that the most interesting case of computing the stable homotopy groups of the spheres corresponds to \(p = 2\).

    I also implemented Yoneda products, obtaining the following example: the multiplicative structure in \(E_2\) related to a homotopy orbit (virtual) representation sphere spectrum that originates from the study of the homotopy type of spaces of long knots, but representation spheres can be thought of as ubiquitous in the equivariant context. The \(E_r-\)Massey products were not considered, although they are straightforward to implement for \(r > 2\).
